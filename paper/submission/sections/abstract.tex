Clinical-trial statistical programming---generating CDISC-conforming SDTM and ADaM
datasets, regulatory tables, and validation artefacts---is a high-stakes,
domain-specialised software task absent from existing code-LLM benchmarks. We
introduce \textbf{ClinProg-Bench}, a public benchmark of \textbf{250} expert-curated
tasks across five categories (code generation, code review, specification
extraction, documentation, and debugging), grounded in the publicly redistributable
CDISC SDTM/ADaM Pilot Project and scored by deterministic, language-agnostic
oracles. The benchmark introduces a stateful runner contract that supports
multi-agent and retrieval-augmented agents, a two-channel leakage audit
(SHA-256 byte overlap + 13-gram Jaccard, both reporting zero hits), and a
dual-reviewer human audit on a stratified random subset (\textbf{Cohen's $\kappa
\geq 0.8$}). Three frontier models evaluated under zero-shot and
fixture-grounded conditions achieve macro scores of 0.22--0.28, with no model
exceeding 0.35 on code generation and scoring near zero on log review and
debugging---establishing substantial headroom for future systems. We release
the corpus under CC-BY-4.0, the toolkit under Apache-2.0, and a Zenodo-archived
snapshot (DOI withheld for double-blind review).
